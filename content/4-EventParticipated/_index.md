# Events Participated

During my internship period and my participation in technology community activities, I joined **4 events** related to **First Cloud AI Journey, AWS, AI, Cloud Architecture, DevOps, Data Analytics, and personal development skills**. Each event gave me a different perspective: from effective self-learning methods, prompt engineering, cloud thinking, and hackathon-based product development to high-scale system design and practical career orientation.

These events not only helped me gain new technical knowledge, but also helped me improve my critical thinking, questioning skills, ability to observe real-world problems, and clearer career orientation in the technology field.

---

### [Event 1](4.1-Event1/)

&emsp;**Event name:** FCAJ Community Day

&emsp;**Time:** May 09, 2026

&emsp;**Location:** First Cloud AI Journey / FCAJ community event

&emsp;**Role in the event:** Participant

&emsp;**Brief description:**  
This event focused on self-learning methods, dopamine management, advanced prompt engineering, career orientation for information technology students, and software development methods combined with AI Agents. The most notable sessions included techniques for “hacking the brain” to maintain learning motivation, Ultimate Prompt Engineering, long-term career thinking, and the BMX/BMM Method for organizing workflows with AI Agents.

&emsp;**Value gained:**  
Through this event, I understood that effective learning does not only depend on the amount of time spent, but also on how the learning environment is designed, how goals are broken down, and how discipline is maintained. In addition, I learned how to build prompts with a clear structure, how to ask “Why” when designing technical solutions, and how to view AI as a tool that supports human thinking rather than a tool that completely replaces humans.

### [Event 2](4.2-Event2/)

&emsp;**Event name:** AI, CloudFront and Multi-Agent System Event

&emsp;**Time:** 08:30 - 12:00, May 23, 2026

&emsp;**Location:** First Cloud AI Journey / FCAJ community event

&emsp;**Role in the event:** Participant

&emsp;**Brief description:**  
This event consisted of multiple sessions about AI, CloudFront, hackathons, AI assistants, LLM non-determinism, and multi-agent systems. The event started with the role of context in using AI effectively, then expanded to a case study on building the UTMorpho product during LotusHacks, how CloudFront optimizes performance and security, AI assistant tools, the non-deterministic behavior of LLMs, and the use of multi-agent systems in startup credit scoring.

&emsp;**Value gained:**  
After attending this event, I realized that the quality of AI output depends heavily on the context, background data, and constraints provided by the user. I also gained a better understanding of CloudFront’s role in modern cloud architecture, how hackathons help develop product thinking, and why real-world LLM deployments require logging, testing, guardrails, and risk-control mechanisms.

### [Event 3](4.3-Event3/)

&emsp;**Event name:** FCAJ Community Day

&emsp;**Time:** May 30, 2026

&emsp;**Location:** First Cloud AI Journey / FCAJ community event

&emsp;**Role in the event:** Participant

&emsp;**Brief description:**  
This event focused on learning AWS in a more practical and safer way through **AWS Cloud Quest** and **Floci**. The session presented common difficulties faced by AWS beginners, such as fear of unexpected costs, forgetting to delete resources, or lacking confidence when deploying cloud services. AWS Cloud Quest was introduced as a gamified learning environment, while Floci was presented as an AWS service emulator that supports local testing. In addition, the event also included sessions about hackathons, confidence, migration from a current stack to AWS, and the importance of DevOps before a project encounters serious incidents.

&emsp;**Value gained:**  
This event helped me form a more reasonable AWS learning roadmap: starting with architectural thinking through Cloud Quest, performing quick local testing with Floci, and then deploying to real AWS environments. I also realized that emulation tools are suitable for learning, demos, and prototypes, but they cannot completely replace real production environments. Besides cloud knowledge, the event also helped me understand the role of confidence and DevOps skills in building technology products.

### [Event 4](4.4-Event4/)

&emsp;**Event name:** FCAJ Community Day - June 2026 Edition

&emsp;**Time:** June 13, 2026

&emsp;**Location:** First Cloud AI Journey / FCAJ community event

&emsp;**Role in the event:** Participant

&emsp;**Brief description:**  
This event provided more in-depth and practical content about system architecture, Data Analytics, DevOps, and career development. One of the highlighted topics was the architecture of a large-scale URL shortener on AWS, using a Key Generation Service, Redis, DynamoDB, ECS, CloudFront, and AWS WAF. In addition, the event included sharing sessions about the real work of Data Analytics Engineers in multinational corporations, no-blame post-mortem thinking, the DevOps career landscape in Vietnam, and the importance of technical fundamentals such as Linux, Networking, Programming, and System Thinking.

&emsp;**Value gained:**  
Through this event, I gained a clearer understanding of how a high-scale system can be designed by separating read and write paths, pre-computing resources, and using cache to reduce latency. I also learned that DevOps is not only about CI/CD or Docker/Kubernetes, but also about operational thinking, observability, security, cost control, and system recovery after incidents. Moreover, the Data Analytics sessions helped me understand that data only becomes valuable when it is analyzed in the right context and communicated as a meaningful story for business decision-making.

## Overall Summary

Overall, the 4 events helped me accumulate knowledge across multiple layers. At the foundation level, I learned how to study more effectively, how to use AI with proper control, and how to create clear and structured prompts. At the technical level, I was exposed to important topics such as AWS CloudFront, Cloud Quest, Floci, Amazon Bedrock, DynamoDB, Redis, ECS, CloudWatch, DevOps, and multi-agent systems. At the career-thinking level, I realized that a technology engineer does not only need to know how to code, but also needs to understand systems, users, trade-offs, collaboration, and responsibility for the products they build.

These events also motivated me to participate more actively in technology communities, learn from experienced professionals, and improve my professional communication skills. They were valuable experiences during my internship period and became an important foundation for shaping my future development path in Cloud, AI, and Software Engineering.
